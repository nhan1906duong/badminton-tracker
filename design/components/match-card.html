Match Card

Displays a badminton doubles match — single set to 21. Two states: Live (during play) and Ended (final scores). Winner is always obvious at a glance.

Anatomy

The card is structured as a horizontal face-off. Left team vs right team, with the score as the visual anchor in the center.

Don't

Huy + Dat

"Win" badge floats with no team connection. Both teams styled identically. Score center is just numbers — hard to scan.

Design Notes

  • The score center is always the visual anchor. Largest type, centered, draws the eye first.
  • Winner emphasis uses three signals in parallel: accent color on score, bold weight on name, full opacity on player names. Loser gets muted grey and lighter weight.
  • W / L indicator sits directly under the score. Winner shows W in accent. Loser shows L in muted. Provides instant outcome recognition.
  • Status badge is positioned on the winning team's side in the meta bar. When the logged-in user's team wins, show Win. When they lose, show Loss. In a neutral view, show Done or omit.
  • Live state uses a 2px accent border + pulsing dot. The current point score is shown prominently.
  • Serving indicator is a small dot next to the serving team's label. Pulses to draw attention during active play.
  • Compact variant removes W/L indicator and reduces padding. Keep winner/loser color distinction — never compromise that.
  • Matches are single set to 21 points, win by 2. Rally point scoring. The main score shows actual points (e.g. 21:18), not set counts.
